Dan Turèll

Dan Turèll (1946–1993), known as "Onkel Danny", was a highly popular Danish author, poet and journalist. Known for his distinctive style and love for Copenhagen, he became one of Denmark’s most recognisable literary voices.

Born and raised in Vangede, Turèll wrote extensively about Copenhagen and everyday city life. His works blended poetry, reportage and personal reflection, creating a unique voice that captured the rhythm of the Danish capital. He published nearly 100 books during his lifetime.

Turèll was deeply inspired by American beat poetry and jazz culture. Today he remains an iconic figure in Danish cultural history — celebrated for his vivid portrayal of Copenhagen’s streets, people and nightlife.

His grave at Assistens Cemetery has become a cultural landmark, visited by readers from around the world who continue to follow in the footsteps of “Onkel Danny”.
